Client Overview

HashKey Group is Asia's leading digital asset financial services group, headquartered in Hong Kong, with a complete Web3 infrastructure ecosystem in Singapore, Japan, Bermuda, and other regions. As one of Hong Kong's first licensed institutions to obtain a Virtual Asset Trading Platform license (full Type 1/4/7/9 licenses), HashKey Group has built a full-stack compliant Web3 financial system covering trading, asset management, on/off-ramp services, and on-chain services.

Challenges & Pain Points

Core Challenges

❶ Single-Dimensional Brand Perception
The market generally perceives HashKey as "just a licensed exchange," lacking systematic understanding of its full-stack financial group positioning and multi-business matrix (exchange + OTC + chain + asset management). Users don't understand the essential difference between HashKey and Coinbase, nor its strategic position in Asia's compliant financial infrastructure.
❷ Lack of Executive Personal IP
As a licensed institution, HashKey's executive team has deep financial and compliance backgrounds, but lacks personal brand influence in the Web3 community. Compared to the active voices of competitors like Binance's He Yi and OKX executives, HashKey executives have an obvious shortfall in industry discourse power.
❸ Fragmented Narrative System
Lack of a unified brand narrative framework. In external communications, business lines such as exchange, OTC, HashKey Chain, and asset management operate independently, failing to form an overall cognitive anchor of "compliant full-stack financial infrastructure," resulting in severe undervaluation of brand value.
❹ Missing Sentiment Monitoring Mechanism
As a licensed institution, HashKey faces higher compliance and reputation risks. However, it lacks a systematic sentiment monitoring system, unable to track market cognitive biases, misunderstandings, and negative voices in real-time, making it difficult to respond promptly and adjust communication strategies.

Strategy Overview

Strategic Positioning

Through a four-in-one approach of "narrative reconstruction + executive IP + KOL matrix + intelligent sentiment monitoring," upgrade HashKey from a "licensed exchange" to the industry cognitive anchor of "Eastern Coinbase+ full-stack financial infrastructure."

Execution Highlights

Highlight 1: Narrative Reconstruction — From "Licensed Exchange" to "Eastern Coinbase+"

Problem: Single-dimensional market perception, users only know HashKey as a "Hong Kong licensed exchange," unaware of its full-stack financial group positioning, severely undervaluing brand value.

Methodology: 6-Step Narrative Construction System

  • Step 1 Research & Goal Setting: Through industry benchmarking analysis, establish "Eastern Coinbase+" positioning, emphasizing "licensed like Coinbase, but doing far more than trading"
  • Step 2 Multi-Angle Breakdown: Design 5 narrative chapters (Group, On/Off-Ramp, Ecosystem, Scenarios, Institutional)
  • Step 3 Layered Storytelling: Progressive three-layer approach of Awareness → Understanding → Trust, Week 1 builds awareness, Week 2 addresses pain points, Week 3 builds trust
  • Step 4 Material Package Creation: Provide KOLs with basic graphic templates, logo/color palette, business structure diagrams, three-sentence pitch, meme materials
  • Step 5 KOL Collaboration: Select 27+ top Chinese-speaking KOLs, including research-focused (Yue Xiaoyu, Yueya.eth, Bocai), investment research leaders (Dayu, AB Kuai.Dong), traders (Chuanmu), narrative amplifiers (Dov)
  • Step 6 Rhythm Advancement: 4-week complete cycle, one content theme per week

Results:

  • "Eastern Coinbase+" narrative organically spread in the community, becoming a meme-like slogan
  • "Compliance is the product" expression quoted by multiple KOLs, enhancing brand stickiness
  • Narrative extended from "licensed exchange" to "RWA/institutional DeFi/stablecoin settlement layer," highlighting global liquidity bridge positioning

Highlight 2: Executive IP Building — From "Behind-the-Scenes Team" to "Industry Discourse Power"

Problem: HashKey's executive team has deep financial and compliance backgrounds, but lacks personal brand influence in the Web3 community, struggling to compete with executives from Binance's He Yi, OKX, and others for industry discourse power.

Methodology: Executive IP Building System (6 Archetypes)

  • Archetype 1 Personal Narrative: Use "personal growth + industry trends + project differentiation" three-part template to establish "female executive representative in the industry" persona
  • Archetype 2 Executive Vision: Design CEO letter-style short posts + external links, speaking out during HashKey new business launches and Hong Kong policy breakthroughs
  • Archetype 3 Light Interaction Series: Establish serialized columns (#HashKeyNotes, #AsiaCryptoPulse, #WomenInCrypto)
  • Archetype 4 Product Analysis: Multi-angle breakdown of HashKey business, using "industry pain point → HashKey solution → philosophy conclusion" structure
  • Archetype 5 Methodology: Output collectible tool-type content, such as "Three Principles of Compliant Exchanges" and "Five Major Trends in Asian Crypto"
  • Archetype 6 Authority Endorsement: Leverage external scenarios like ReutersNEXT stablecoin interviews to strengthen "compliance + Asia + female leadership" persona

Results:

  • Established differentiated executive persona of "compliance + Asia + female leadership"
  • Formed reusable content template library (CEO letters, three-part threads, methodology checklists, light interaction polls)
  • Laid systematic framework for long-term IP operations

Highlight 4: Sentiment Monitoring & IPO Crisis Defense — Building 24/7 Reputation Management System

Problem: As a licensed institution, HashKey faces higher compliance and reputation risks. During the critical IPO period, the market is highly sensitive to token prices, financial data, buyback plans, etc. Lacking a systematic sentiment monitoring and crisis response mechanism makes it difficult to respond promptly to market questions.

Methodology: Daily Sentiment Intelligence System + IPO PR Crisis Defense

  • Daily Sentiment Monitoring: Establish daily sentiment report mechanism, real-time tracking of social media, KOL discussions, community feedback, identifying potential risk points and positive communication opportunities
  • IPO Crisis Defense Checklist: Based on prospectus details, compiled a complete set of PR crisis defensive answer list, covering core questions (HSK buyback, token usage rate) and derivative questions (revenue processing, digital asset composition)
  • Strategic Response Principles: Proactively avoid sensitive data such as financial data and operational performance, avoid focusing Web3 participants' attention too much on costs, expenses, expenditures and other financial fundamentals; emphasize more on the company's long-term cultivation and business development strategic plans
  • Dynamic Adjustment Mechanism: Combined with daily sentiment monitoring, flexibly supplement new questions and responses, ensuring rapid response capability during critical IPO roadshow period

Results:

  • Established systematic daily sentiment monitoring system, achieving 7x24 reputation risk early warning
  • Built complete IPO PR crisis defense Q&A library, covering core and derivative questions
  • Formed strategic response framework, effectively guiding market focus from short-term financial data to long-term strategic value
  • Provided real-time support for sentiment management during IPO roadshow period, ensuring brand reputation stability

IPO PR Crisis Defense Checklist (Sample Examples)

Core Question: HSK Buyback Plan

Question: The company mentioned using 20% of group net profit to buyback and burn HSK, when is profitability expected?

Response Strategy: The company will strictly comply with Hong Kong securities regulatory requirements, and after comprehensive evaluation of company operations, capital structure, and market environment, execute the buyback plan at an appropriate time. The core support for HSK value is the company's trading platform competitiveness and on-chain ecosystem.

Core Question: HSK Usage Rate Decline

Question: HSK usage rate decreased from 1.71% at end of 2024 to 0.49% in mid-2025, how will the company respond?

Response Strategy: An important factor in the decline of HSK usage rate is the sluggish cryptocurrency market, but from a long-term perspective there is still huge incremental space. With IPO fundraising completion, the company will focus on developing new products and expanding new businesses, injecting new vitality into HSK.

Core Question: Paying Fees with HSK

Question: From 2022-2025 H1, approximately HK$380M in service fees were paid in HSK form, will this continue to increase in the future?

Response Strategy: Historically the company has paid supplier service fees in HSK form. After IPO fundraising completion, the company's cash will be further supplemented. Future funds will be more invested in infrastructure construction, market development, etc., and this investment will use raised cash rather than HSK.

Derivative Question: Revenue Processing

Question: The company estimates 5% HSK usage rate, is this estimated consumption directly recorded as current period revenue?

Response Strategy: The company's accounting treatment of HSK complies with International Financial Reporting Standards, with no "beautification of financial reports" or inflated revenue. When users use HSK to offset transaction fees, the company confirms revenue based on usage rate. The amount recorded as revenue during historical periods is very small, with minimal impact on company revenue.

Defense Checklist Coverage: 4 core questions (HSK buyback, paying fees with HSK, newly distributed HSK, HSK usage rate) + 2 derivative questions (revenue processing, digital asset composition), forming complete IPO sentiment defense system
